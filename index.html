On ellipses


a special clue type you might encounter are themed double definitions.

they will always be split by an ellipsis (...) and end with a question mark.

the first part of the clue is a normal definition.

the second part of the clue needs to be interpreted in the context of the puzzle's theme.

for example, let's say that the theme of the puzzle is REMOVED:

Our planet... from scarcity? (5)

"our planet" = EARTH

"scarcity" = DEARTH  ➡ EARTH

since the theme is telling us to remove "D" (REMOVE-D), DEARTH represents EARTH within this context.



Controls


first, click or tap any node to select it and its parent word.

then, use the arrow keys or tap to navigate within the word.

use the keyboard to insert symbols.

reselect any intersecting node (or press "enter" on desktop) to toggle between words.

press "tab" to cycle through the word list.

press "hint" to reveal the selected node.

press "check" when you're done.
